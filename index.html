The Town That Forgot Its Own Name

By Arjun S. Mehta 3 min read 12 comments 47 likes
🏛️
₹ 2.50
BHARAT
05 MAR
2026
14:22
↑ The unnamed street — photographed on expired Kodak film, circa last Tuesday.

There is a street in the old quarter that the map has forgotten. The rickshaw-wallahs know it by feel alone — a left past the broken clock, a right where the smell of cardamom grows thick as fog. Nobody has agreed on its name in forty years, and somehow, that is perfectly fine.

I found it on a rainy November morning when I was looking for something else entirely. That is the only way such places are ever found — sideways, through the corner of the eye, when you have stopped insisting on being anywhere in particular.

An old woman was frying jalebis outside a blue door that had been painted and repainted so many times the wood had forgotten it was ever wood. She did not ask my name. I did not ask hers. We shared a silence comfortable as an old shawl, and that was enough of an introduction.

Letters My Grandfather Never Sent

By Arjun S. Mehta Video · 8 min 5 comments 89 likes
📽️
₹ 5.00
BHARAT
08 MAR
2026
19:45
▶
[ CLICK TO PLAY — Reel No. 7 · Super 8 Transfer ]

In the bottom of a tin trunk that smelled of camphor and old rain, my grandfather kept thirty-seven envelopes, each addressed in his careful hand, none of them ever posted. To villages. To cities. To people who had since become memory.

I filmed the reading of three of them last November, with the light going golden through the shuttered window of his old room. The camera caught the dust motes and, I think, something else too — something that does not translate into language but lives just fine on film.
