5'O Clock PM

By The Ruh 11 Mar 2026 5 min read
🌅
₹ 2.50
BHARAT
11 MAR
2026
17:00

Somewhere around 5:00 PM, engines across the world begin to slow down. Not just machines — people too. In many cultures, evening is simply another part of the day. But in Indian life, evening has always felt like a quiet celebration. It is the hour when the day slowly releases us.

The hour when we return to our own small personal spaces. It feels like a long sigh after holding our breath all day. Sometimes it makes me wonder — do we truly love the professions we chose, or are we simply running inside a silent rat race, imprisoned by time?

We give it many names. But whatever we call it, sometimes it quietly exhausts us. I mention India not to limit the idea, but because I write from what I have lived. My memories belong to this land, to the evenings I grew up inside.

Yet the sky above us is the same everywhere. The sky only knows sunrise and sunset. It does not know our borders, professions, or worries. Every evening, the sky gently changes its colors — as if it is writing poetry on itself.

In childhood, evenings during school life gave us a special kind of happiness. Some children remember joy. Some remember pressure. Some remember loneliness. Some remember growth.

My strongest memories are simple ones. Rushing home from school. Sitting in front of the television. Cartoons and shows filled my evenings: Chhota Bheem, Tom and Jerry, Scooby-Doo, Mr. Bean, Penguins of Madagascar and many others. Those small moments felt like entire worlds.

But they became memories. And memories have a strange power. They slowly heal parts of us that we didn't even know were hurt.

Evening becomes a release from the world. It becomes the time when life begins again. People listen to music. Write poetry. Draw pictures. Attend dance classes. Passions quietly wake up during the evening.

Perhaps that is why evening feels so beautiful — it is the moment when life gently says: You survived the day. Now live a little.

I am theRuh, a storyteller. This is only the beginning. There are many more evening thoughts waiting to be written. If this thought resonated with you, stay awhile. More stories will arrive with the next evening. Until then, have a beautiful life.
