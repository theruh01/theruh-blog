5 O’Clock PM
Somewhere around 5:00 PM, engines across the world begin to slow down.
Not just machines — people too.
In many cultures, evening is simply another part of the day.
But in Indian life, evening has always felt like a quiet celebration.
It is the hour when the day slowly releases us.
The hour when we return to our own small personal spaces.
It feels like a long sigh after holding our breath all day.
Sometimes it makes me wonder —
do we truly love the professions we chose,
or are we simply running inside a silent rat race, imprisoned by time?
We give it many names.
But whatever we call it, sometimes it quietly exhausts us.
I mention India not to limit the idea,
but because I write from what I have lived.
My memories belong to this land,
to the evenings I grew up inside.
Yet the sky above us is the same everywhere.
The sky only knows sunrise and sunset.
It does not know our borders, professions, or worries.
Every evening, the sky gently changes its colors —
as if it is writing poetry on itself.
And everyone knows this feeling.
Evening always carries a different breath
from the rest of the day.
In Childhood
Evenings during childhood were different.
School life gave evenings a special kind of happiness.
But school experiences are never the same for everyone.
Just like life itself.
Some children remember joy.
Some remember pressure.
Some remember loneliness.
Some remember growth.
But as a saying goes:
“Whatever it was, what was meant to be will be.”
For most children, evening meant playtime.
Running outside.
Playing games.
Laughing with friends.
But my evenings were a little different.
My strongest memories are simple ones.
Rushing home from school.
Sometimes still wearing my uniform.
Sitting in front of the television.
Cartoons and shows filled my evenings:
Chhota Bheem
Tom and Jerry
Scooby-Doo
Mr. Bean
Penguins of Madagascar
and many others.
Those small moments felt like entire worlds.
I rarely went outside to play.
Instead, I watched stories on television or movies on CD.
Sometimes I sat in the portico of our rented house, playing games on my tablet.
Simple evenings.
Quiet evenings.
But they became memories.
And memories have a strange power.
They slowly heal parts of us that we didn't even know were hurt.
As we grow older, evenings change their meaning.
After school…
after college…
after work…
Evening becomes a release from the world.
It becomes the time when life begins again.
People listen to music.
Write poetry.
Draw pictures.
Attend dance classes.
Go to tuition.
Passions quietly wake up during the evening.
It is as if 5:00 PM refreshes the human spirit.
The world slows down,
and our inner selves finally begin to breathe.
Perhaps that is why evening feels so beautiful.
It is the moment when life gently says:
You survived the day.
Now live a little.
